}

Santa has provided you a list of the weights of every package he needs to fit on the sleigh. The packages need to be split into three groups of exactly the same weight, and every package has to fit.
The first group - the one going in the passenger compartment - needs as few packages as possible so that Santa has some legroom left over.
If there are multiple ways to arrange the packages such that the fewest possible are in the first group, choose the way where the first group has the smallest quantum entanglement. The quantum entanglement of a group of packages is the product of their weights - the value you get when you multiply their weights together.
